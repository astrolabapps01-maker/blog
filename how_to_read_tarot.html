How to Start Tarot Reading as a Beginner: A Complete Guide to Understanding the Cards

Every tarot expert was once someone who picked up their very first deck and wondered, "What does this card really mean?"

Tarot is often surrounded by mystery, but at its heart, it is a powerful tool for self-reflection, intuition, and personal growth. You don't need psychic abilities or years of spiritual practice to learn how to start tarot reading as a beginner. All you need is an open mind, curiosity, and the willingness to learn.

Whether you're looking for daily guidance, clarity in relationships, or insight into career decisions, this beginner's guide will help you take your first step into the fascinating world of tarot.

What Is Tarot Reading?

Tarot reading is the practice of interpreting a deck of 78 symbolic cards to gain perspective on life's questions and situations. Rather than predicting a fixed, unalterable future, tarot encourages deep self-awareness and helps you explore possibilities, emotions, and choices.

Many people use tarot as a daily mindfulness ritual, while others turn to it during moments of uncertainty or major life transitions.

Understanding the Tarot Deck Layout

A standard tarot deck contains 78 cards, divided neatly into two foundational structural groups that build your reading foundation.

Major Arcana (22 Cards)

These primary cards represent monumental life lessons, spiritual growth milestones, and significant karmic events. When you learn Major Arcana cards meaning systems, you understand overarching themes taking place in your reality.

Some famous Major Arcana cards include:

  • The Fool – New beginnings, clean slates, and adventure
  • The Magician – Confidence, personal willpower, and manifestation
  • The High Priestess – Intuition, sub-conscious secrets, and inner wisdom
  • The Lovers – Relationships, alignment, and meaningful choices
  • The Star – Hope, recovery, healing, and inspiration
  • The World – Completion, global cycles, and achievement

Whenever a Major Arcana card appears in a layout, it highlights a major energetic theme influencing your current timeline.

Minor Arcana (56 Cards)

These cards focus on temporary, everyday experiences and are divided into four specific suits. Applying basic Minor Arcana suit interpretations helps reveal day-to-day practical choices:

  • Cups ❤️ – Represents changing emotions, relationships, intuition, and love.
  • Pentacles 💰 – Represents career changes, finances, health, and material success.
  • Swords ⚔️ – Represents mental thoughts, communication, challenges, and truth.
  • Wands 🔥 – Represents raw creativity, passion, ambition, and personal growth.

Each individual suit contains cards numbered Ace through Ten, plus four specialized Court Cards: Page, Knight, Queen, and King.

Choosing Your First Tarot Deck

Many beginners mistakenly believe that someone must gift them their first physical tarot deck. In reality, you can absolutely select and buy one yourself.

When searching for a deck, look for options that check these boxes:

  • Has artwork you genuinely enjoy looking at
  • Uses clear, easily recognizable classic symbolism
  • Includes a helpful beginner-friendly guidebook
  • Feels comfortable, comforting, and inspiring to handle

The standard, time-tested **Rider Waite Smith tarot deck for beginners** remains the globally recommended option because thousands of online learning resources, apps, and books are built directly around its imagery.

How to Prepare for Your First Reading

You don't need expensive candles, crystals, or elaborate setups to conduct an impactful session. Instead, follow these simple preparation phases:

  1. Find a quiet, physically comfortable place
  2. Take a few deep, stabilizing breaths
  3. Clear your mind of external daily anxieties
  4. Focus cleanly on a specific question or intention

Simple, open questions work best. Instead of asking closed yes-or-no questions right away, test phrases like: "What should I focus on today?" or "What energy surrounds my current career path?"

Your First One Card Daily Tarot Reading Spread

The easiest spread for any novice is the one card daily tarot reading pull. Simply shuffle your deck thoroughly while visualizing your question, and draw a single card.

Before reading the companion guide book, ask yourself:

  • What immediate emotion does this illustration create?
  • Which specific symbols catch your eye first?
  • How might this match what you are going through right now?

Moving to a Three Card Tarot Spread

Once you develop comfort reading single card impressions, step up to easy three card tarot spreads. Lay out three cards horizontally in front of you to read them chronologically:

  • Card 1 (Past): What old energy or situations influenced this current matter?
  • Card 2 (Present): What dynamics are occurring right now?
  • Card 3 (Future): What potential direction is the current path leading toward?

Common Mistakes Beginners Make

Memorizing Every Meaning Instantly: You do not need to struggle over all 78 card attributes instantly. Trusting contextual artwork patterns yields vastly superior results compared to raw memorization.

Asking the Same Question Repeatedly: Pulling consecutive spreads over an identical issue out of frustration causes confusion. Accept the initial pull, step away, and revisit the cards later.

How to Improve Your Intuitive Skills

Consistency is key to mastering this craft. Try cementing these quick habits into your daily schedule:

  • Pull one card every morning using your deck or your mobile tracker.
  • Keep a dedicated physical journal detailing your spreads.
  • Write down initial observations before opening reference definitions.

Every reading provides an incredible opportunity to learn, align your goals, and uncover underlying psychological clarity. Embrace the process, trust your intuition, and let the cards illuminate your path forward!
